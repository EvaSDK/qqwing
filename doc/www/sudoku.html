Instructions for Sudoku

  1. The goal of the game is to choose a number for each square such that no digit appears twice in any row, column, or section.
  2. Start a new game with your desired level of difficulty.
  3. Click on a possibility to choose that number for the square. The possibility will be removed from other squares in the row, column, and section.
  4. Hold down control, alt, or shift to and click on a possibility to remove the possibility from the square, but not mark the square.
  5. Click on an assigned number to remove it from the square and put back any possibilities.
  6. Use the colors however you wish. You may find it helpful to switch the color whenever you guess.

This sudoku board makes solving puzzles much easier, is this cheating?

This Sudoku playing board should be helpful, but it doesn't solve the puzzles for you. It only enforces the basic rule of Sudoku: you may not put the same number twice into any row, column, or section. Even with this helpful page, it is still possible to choose numbers such that the puzzle can not be solved. Here, all the bookkeeping is done by the Sudoku playing board, freeing your brain up to do the more challenging leaps of logic. Even so, purists may want to stick to pencil and paper.
